Exploring Indian Culture in Malaysia

Discover the rich cultural heritage and vibrant traditions of the Indian community in Malaysia.

Language

The Indian community in Malaysia speaks a variety of languages, including **Tamil**, **Hindi**, **Punjabi**, **Malayalam**, and **Telugu**. The majority speak **Tamil**, while **English** is widely spoken in urban areas and educational institutions.

Religion

Indian Malaysians practice several religions, including **Hinduism**, **Islam**, **Christianity**, and **Sikhism**. Hinduism is the most widely practiced religion, especially among the Tamil community, with significant celebrations like Deepavali and Thaipusam.

Festivals

Key festivals celebrated by the Indian community include:

  • Deepavali - The Festival of Lights, celebrated with lights, sweets, and prayers.
  • Thaipusam - A religious procession dedicated to Lord Murugan.
  • Pongal - A harvest festival celebrated with food, rituals, and traditional dances.
  • Navaratri - A nine-night festival dedicated to Goddess Durga.
  • Vaisakhi - A harvest festival and the founding of the Sikh community.

Cuisine

Indian cuisine in Malaysia is diverse and flavorful, offering dishes like:

  • Nasi Kandar - A rice dish with curries and meats.
  • Roti Canai - A crispy flatbread served with dhal or curry.
  • Teh Tarik - A sweet, frothy tea drink.
  • Chapati and Paratha - Flatbreads served with curries.
  • Biryani - A fragrant rice dish with meat and spices.

Traditional Clothing

Traditional Indian clothing includes:

  • Saree - A long, draped garment worn by women.
  • Salwar Kameez - A tunic with pants, worn by many women.
  • Lungi - A traditional garment worn by men, especially in the south.
  • Kurta - A long tunic worn by men, usually paired with a pyjama.

Arts & Crafts

Indian Malaysians are known for their traditional arts and crafts such as:

  • Kolam - Decorative patterns drawn at the entrances of homes during festivals.
  • Batik - A textile art used to create beautiful clothing like sarees.
  • Classical Dance - Indian classical dance forms like Bharatanatyam and Kathak.
